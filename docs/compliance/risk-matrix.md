# PennyForge — Compliance Risk Matrix

> **Not legal advice.** This is a practical risk framework and set of product guardrails, written for counsel to review before launch.

This matrix enumerates the legal, privacy, and reputational risks counsel should review before
launch, mapped to the concrete guardrails that exist in this repo today. It extends
`docs/compliance.md`; the canonical source policy it repeatedly leans on is
`docs/compliance/data-sources.md`. Rows are ordered highest severity first (impact-weighted, then
likelihood). US-market assumptions throughout; state-specific questions are flagged as "for
counsel."

Scope reminder: PennyForge surfaces community-reported clearance and penny prices from first-hand,
in-store observation. It does not scrape, does not touch retailer systems, and does not teach
anyone how to manipulate them. Several rows below exist precisely to keep that true.

## Risk matrix

| Risk | Scenario | Likelihood | Impact | Product guardrail / mitigation | Residual risk for counsel |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| In-community illegal or abusive behavior spillover | Users coordinate tag swapping (which is fraud, full stop), shelf-clearing sweeps, or harassment/pressuring of store employees, and PennyForge is portrayed as the venue that enabled it. | Med | High | Community policy prohibits price-tag tampering, harassing or deceiving employees, and coordinated shelf-clearing; the app footer (`app/layout.tsx`) tells users to be kind to store employees; ADMIN/CAPTAIN moderation queue (`/admin`) can reject reports; free-text `Report.notes` is moderated content. Product framing never presents leads as techniques for manipulating retailer systems. | Draft enforceable community guidelines + ToS with termination rights; review whether any state's organized-retail-crime statutes create platform exposure; define a moderation SLA before Phase 2 scale. |
| Privacy: home location and receipt PII | `User.homeLat`/`homeLng` (see `prisma/schema.prisma`) leaks or is subpoenaed; Phase 1 receipt uploads carry card last-4, loyalty numbers, or names and get displayed publicly. | Med | High | MVP: home location distance math happens in `lib/routePlanner.ts` (computes `distanceMiles` from `homeLat`/`homeLng`, using `lib/geo.ts#haversineMiles`), which feeds `lib/route.ts` for scoring; it is never shown to other users; evidence is a placeholder URL, no file upload exists yet; only email + handle are collected beyond location. Phase 1 (file upload, per `docs/product-spec.md`) must add receipt PII redaction before any public display, plus retention limits. | Data-retention and deletion policy needed before Phase 1; breach-notification obligations by state; whether lat/lng at home-address precision should be coarsened at rest ("for counsel" + engineering). |
| State privacy statutes (CCPA/CPRA, VCDPA, etc.) and COPPA | PennyForge crosses a state statute's applicability threshold, or children under 13 create accounts. | Med | High | MVP collects minimal PII (email, handle, optional home ZIP/lat/lng) and sells none of it. Mock auth (`lib/currentUser.ts`) has no age gate today; Phase 1 real auth must add a 13+ gate at signup and a privacy policy with state-required disclosures/rights requests. | For counsel: CCPA/CPRA revenue/records thresholds and when PennyForge trips them; whether geolocation at this precision is "sensitive personal information" under CPRA; COPPA position if under-13 use is discovered despite the gate. |
| CFAA / unauthorized-access adjacent claims | A retailer alleges PennyForge accessed systems without authorization. | Low | High | No private or undocumented endpoints, no credentialed scraping, no reverse engineering, no rate-limit bypass — all forbidden in `docs/compliance/data-sources.md` and structurally absent from the codebase; `PRIVATE_API` and `AUTOMATED_TOOL` submissions are rejected server-side by `assertSafeSource()` (`lib/compliance.ts`, covered by `tests/compliance.test.ts`). All data is first-hand human observation in public retail spaces. | Confirm the guardrail survives Phase 1+ integrations (official APIs only, under published terms with counsel sign-off per the data-sources process). |
| Retailer ToS breach claims | A retailer claims PennyForge's data collection violates its website/app terms. | Low | High | PennyForge never ingests retailer websites or apps: the code allowlist accepts only the four first-hand in-store source types, `SCRAPED_SITE` is explicitly blocked, and unknown source strings are rejected by default (allowlist, not denylist). User-entered product URLs are stored as pointers and never auto-fetched. | Website ToS generally do not govern a shopper's own in-store observations, but counsel should confirm no retailer's terms purport to restrict sharing in-store price information. |
| IP/copyright claims over competitor lists | A paid penny-list vendor claims PennyForge reposted its curated lists or feeds. | Low | High | `COMPETITOR_REPOST` is a blocked source type with a specific rejection message; gray-provenance UPC/inventory databases are forbidden; manually curated lists must be built only from first-hand reports already in the system (see `docs/compliance/data-sources.md`). Independent first-hand reports that happen to overlap a competitor's list are not copies. | DMCA agent registration before launch; takedown process for the rare case a user pastes competitor content into `Report.notes`. |
| Defamation in user notes/comments | A user's free-text note accuses a store or employee of misconduct; the store or individual threatens suit. | Med | Med | `Report.notes` is the only free-text surface in the MVP; reports default to `PENDING` and pass through the ADMIN/CAPTAIN moderation queue before approval; community guidelines prohibit naming or targeting individual employees. | Section 230 posture for UGC (platform vs. publisher) — keep moderation content-neutral; notice-and-takedown workflow for defamation complaints. |
| Retailer cease-and-desist / PR pressure despite full compliance | A retailer objects to clearance-price visibility itself and sends a C&D or runs press against "penny hunter apps," even though no line was crossed. | Med | Med | The entire architecture is the mitigation: documented allowlist enforcement (`lib/compliance.ts` + `tests/compliance.test.ts`), no scraping/automation to point at, per-page disclaimers, and this documentation suite as a ready-made good-faith record. Response posture is cooperative, not defiant. | Pre-draft a C&D response playbook with counsel; decide in advance which (if any) retailer requests — e.g., delisting a chain — the business would honor. |
| Trademark misuse / implied retailer affiliation | Use of retailer names (`Retailer` model seeds Home Depot, Lowe's, Dollar General, Walmart) reads as endorsement or affiliation. | Med | Med | Footer disclaimer in `app/layout.tsx` states PennyForge is not affiliated with any retailer; retailer names are used nominatively (to identify where a deal is), no retailer logos or trade dress are shipped in the repo. | Nominative fair use review; marketing/app-store screenshots must repeat the non-affiliation disclaimer; avoid retailer names in ad keywords without counsel review. |
| App store rejection | Apple/Google reviewers flag the Phase 4 native shell as enabling objectionable commerce behavior or misreading it as a scraping tool. | Med | Med | Web-first roadmap (`docs/product-spec.md`) means store review risk is deferred to Phase 4; framing rules (community-reported prices, no system-manipulation language) apply to store listings too. See the sibling deep-dive `docs/compliance/app-store-risk.md` for guideline-by-guideline analysis. | Reviewer-notes packet explaining the UGC + compliance model; contingency: PWA distribution if a listing stalls. |
| UGC accuracy harms (wrong prices, stale leads) | A user drives across town for a lead that is wrong or already gone and blames PennyForge. | High | Low | Confidence scoring with a visible breakdown (`lib/scoring.ts`, shown on the lead detail page); freshness decay; dead-vote suppression (2+ dead votes outnumbering confirms removes the lead from feed, alerts via `lib/alerts.ts`, and route planner); price sanity bounds (1¢–$5,000) in `validateReportInput()`; footer disclaimer that nothing is guaranteed and prices vary by store. | Confirm disclaimer wording is sufficient to defeat reliance-based claims; keep it on every price-bearing page in production. |

## Reading and maintaining this matrix

- **Severity method:** rows are sorted by impact first, then likelihood. Likelihood assumes the
  guardrail column holds; if a guardrail is weakened, re-score the row before shipping.
- **Guardrails must stay concrete.** Every mitigation above points at code, schema, or a named
  policy document. When a Phase 1+ feature lands (real auth, file upload, push notifications),
  update the affected rows — privacy, COPPA, and app-store rows change the most.
- **Escalation:** if a feature request would flip any Forbidden row in
  `docs/compliance/data-sources.md` or materially raise a High-impact row here, stop and flag it
  (per `CLAUDE.md` hard boundaries) rather than building a workaround.
